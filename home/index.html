Eleftheria Briakou

Ph.D. Student

University of Maryland, College Park

I am a third-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Maryland, College Park. I am a member of the CLIP lab advised by Marine Carpuat. My research interests revolve around the NLP fields of semantics, natural language inference (NLI), multilingual NLP, and neural machine translation (NMT). I am currently focusing on detecting differences in meaning that span multiple languages. My ultimate goal is to explore how—coarse or fine-grained—cross-lingual semantic mismatches question common assumptions related to using data when developing NLP technology within and across languages.

Previously, I interned with Joel Tetreault at Dataminr. Before that, I was a member of the SLP lab, advised by Alexandros Potamianos, where I worked on the extraction of topic-based word embeddings that integrate contextual variations in word semantics. Both Greek and English versions of my thesis can be found here.
